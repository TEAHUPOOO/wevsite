Essential Factors to Consider When Buying a Cast Iron Skillet

Consider these key factors when choosing your cast iron skillet:

  • Size and Weight: Most home cooks find 10-12 inch skillets most versatile. Consider if you can comfortably handle the weight.
  • Cooking Surface: Traditional rough-cast surfaces build seasoning well, while machined surfaces start smoother but may take longer to develop non-stick properties.
  • Handle Design: Look for comfortable grip angles and helper handles for safer handling.
  • Pre-seasoning: Most modern skillets come pre-seasoned, but quality varies significantly between manufacturers.

Frequently Asked Questions

How do I season a cast iron skillet?

Clean the pan thoroughly, dry completely, then apply a thin layer of high-smoke-point oil (like grapeseed or flaxseed). Heat upside down in a 450°F oven for 1 hour, let cool, and repeat 2-3 times for best results.

Can I use soap on cast iron?

Modern dish soap is mild enough to use occasionally on well-seasoned cast iron. Just avoid harsh scrubbing, always dry thoroughly, and apply a light coat of oil after washing.

Why is my cast iron skillet sticky?

Sticky residue usually indicates excess oil in the seasoning process or cooking with too much oil at low temperatures. Strip and reseason the pan, or cook fatty foods at high heat to remedy the issue.

How long does a cast iron skillet last?

With proper care, a quality cast iron skillet can last for generations. Many antique skillets from the early 1900s are still in regular use today.
