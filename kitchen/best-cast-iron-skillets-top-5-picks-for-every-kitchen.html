Frequently Asked Questions

How do I season a cast iron skillet?

Clean the pan thoroughly, apply a thin layer of high-smoke-point oil (like flaxseed or grapeseed), and bake upside down at 450°F for one hour. Repeat 2-3 times for best results.

Can I use soap on cast iron?

Modern dish soap is mild enough to use occasionally on well-seasoned cast iron. Just avoid soaking the pan and dry it thoroughly after washing to prevent rust.

Why is my cast iron skillet sticky?

Sticky residue usually indicates excess oil during seasoning or cooking at too low temperatures. Strip the pan and re-season, or continue cooking with proper heat and oil amounts to improve the surface.

How do I remove rust from cast iron?

Scrub the rust with steel wool or a wire brush, then wash, dry thoroughly, and immediately re-season the pan. For heavy rust, you may need to strip the pan completely first.
